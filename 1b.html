And that's when it happened, she heard the familiar tinkle of Benson, her neighbour's cat. She unconsciously dropped her bike and wandered, as if floating, through the gate towards the sound. Benson was itching his hip against the bark of one of the olive trees and paused in giving Mina a discerning look before waltzing over.

Benson was not always affectionate, and Mina felt a rush as he approached and begin to rub against her, as if she was an olive tree and her legs were lined with fine bark. As she leant down to put him, Benson twitched as if responding to an approaching phantom, and before she could touch the velvetine hair, with a shimmer and tinkle he was gone. Mina looked around left and right, to see where he had gone to but there was no trace. She decided to go...

  • ... look for Benson to the right
  • ... to go back into the garden and wait for Dad
