THE PARADOX OF PLAYING BY YOUR OWN RULES

Life is easy if we play by our own rules. This seems true enough, but we still must create rules, and live by them, to create a rule that changes on a whim is no rule at all. Another aspect of self-help that I have found fruitful is the celebration of self- discipline. The reason this is important is that it helps to avoid the pitfalls of decision fatigue and moral licensing - sticking to your own rule is making a decision once and not having to do it over and over, and not having to find the moral rectitude to do-the-right-thing every time. This leaves mental and moral energy free to be spent in the moment. But why?

Why do we have to impose artificial rules on ourselves?
